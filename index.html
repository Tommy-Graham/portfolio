About Me

Approximately 1 million years ago - or so it seems -, in the Before Times, I moved from a small, nondiscript village in the Midlands, to a city in the North West of England. I did so to study art, as was my want. After graduating, I got myself a studio and worked for years as a painter, illustrator and cartoonist for galleries, publications and private clients. I subsidised this when needed, with various different jobs in hospitality such as bar tender or Front of House Supervisor. Fast forward to today, and I still live in the North West, in a lovely little mid-terrace, with my Wife, our two dogs and an emotionally disturbed cat. You're welcome to pop round and say hello, but do ring in advance, just to make sure we're in.

After years of working as an illustrator and cartoonist, the world finally became weirder and more outlandish than anything you could successfully relay through parody, and I decided to change career. The idea of becoming a developer appealed to me as - through my various incarnations in art and design - I've aqcuired a skillset I can use in a completely different field. I also relish a challenge and like to push myself in to learning new things, so coding seemed like an ideal fit. Additionally, I must have seen the 1995 film 'Hackers' like 15 times, which may have had some residual influence on my decision at a subconscious level.
